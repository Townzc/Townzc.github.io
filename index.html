Dec. 2024 – Present

Manuscript in Preparation

ASGP-SAM-Geo: Robust Interactive Medical Image Segmentation with Semantic Enhancement and Geometric Denoising

Project Leader

Research on interactive abdominal multi-organ segmentation for the FLARE22 benchmark, built on SAM ViT-B, aimed at improving the reliability of vision foundation models in medical imaging.

  • Proposed a progressive framework addressing cross-modal semantic alignment, prompt-noise robustness, scale-aware fairness across organs, and stable optimization under uncertain supervision.
  • Designed a text-guided semantic enhancement module and auxiliary prototype branch to inject anatomical priors into class-agnostic visual representations.
  • Developed a Perturbation Module, Consistency Purifier, and AdaptiveSoftPurifier to estimate interaction reliability and convert hard denoising into continuous optimization.
  • Built a modular PyTorch pipeline with per-sample loss routing and purity-driven prompt caching, introducing no additional inference cost.
